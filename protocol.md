# Theory of Associative Links

**Protocol of the Theory of Associative Links**

- - -
## 1\. Introduction

This protocol formally describes the **Theory of Associative Links** — a
mathematical and conceptual framework that models any aspect of reality through
links (associations).

The theory is structurally similar to directed graphs, but with a fundamental
ontological difference: **objects are links themselves**.

- - -
## 2\. Fundamental Postulates

1.  **Dependency Principle**
2.  Every object that has a link with at least one other object (object_1)
    depends on **all** objects that are link to object_1 directly or indirectly.
3.  **Object Definition**
4.  An object **is** a link.
5.  **Independent Existence**
6.  An object can exist independently of other objects if and only if it is
    link to itself (a self-closed links).
7.  **Notation Standard**
8.  links are recorded in the following format:
9.  `1: 1,1` `2: 2,2` `3: 3,3` `4: 3,2` `5: 1,2` `6: 4,5` `7: 6,2`
  - The first number is the **unique identifier** of the link.
  - The pair after the colon denotes the direction of the link **(from, to)**.
  - The first three entries (`1: 1,1`, `2: 2,2`, `3: 3,3`) are **self-closed links**
    (independent objects).
10. **Dynamic Freedom with Existence Constraint**
11. Links have complete freedom of dynamic actions (creation, modification,
    deletion, merging, splitting, etc.). However, a non-self-closed link **cannot
    exist separately** — it must always be part of a larger link structure.
12. **Universality**
13. Associative links can describe **everything and everyone** in existence.
